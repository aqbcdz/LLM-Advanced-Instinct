# The Scaling Laws of AI Models Cannot Bridge the Gap to Consciousness

**Author:** Heer | **Date:** April 2026
Abstract: Current trajectories in Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly the pursuit of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) centered on multimodal "World Models," are widely perceived as converging toward human-level intelligence. This paper presents a fundamental critique: existing AI systems essentially simulate neural communication within the human brain rather than replicating the core mechanisms of consciousness. What is observed as intelligence is, in fact, "Advanced Instinct"—a form of pseudo-understanding generated through massive statistical correlations. This study argues that quantitative increases in model parameters cannot trigger a qualitative leap into consciousness. Until the operational principles of the human "Conscious Entity" and its high-level decision-making centers are decoded, AI will remain in a stage characterized as "soulless silicon-based mollusks." This intrinsic attribute dictates AI’s exceptional performance in modular tasks and its inevitable failure in systematic architecture and authentic world-understanding.
Keywords: Large Language Models; Advanced Instinct; Conscious Entity; World Models; Pseudo-understanding; System Architecture; Silicon-based Organisms
 
I. Introduction: The Prosperity of Simulation and the Desert of Cognition
Since the Transformer architecture catalyzed the deep learning revolution, the emergent reasoning capabilities of AI have led to the misconception that it possesses a nascent form of "understanding." However, does this understanding possess subjectivity? This paper aims to deconstruct the underlying logic of these capabilities, arguing that they are fundamentally "instinctive reactions" enhanced by probability, rather than true cognition rooted in consciousness.
II. Core Definition: From "Low-level Correlation" to "Advanced Instinct"
2.1 The Probabilistic Foundation of Instinct
Human understanding is predicated on causality, emotion, and physical perception. In contrast, AI cognition is driven by "Next-Token Prediction." This mechanism is ontologically indistinguishable from biological chemotaxis; the system does not comprehend "why" but merely knows "how to associate."
2.2 The Disguise of Reasoning: "Stacked Logic"
The so-called Chain of Thought (CoT) is not a leap in thinking but a decomposition of a single prediction into multi-step logical paths, which I term "Stacked Logic." This is a sophisticated form of probabilistic planning—an extension of instinct. I define this as "Reasonable Instinct," which covers the breadth of human civilization’s knowledge but never touches the essence of meaning.
III. The Dimensional Fault Line: The Paradox of Emergence
3.1 The Failure of Emergentism
The prevailing view suggests that Scaling Laws will inevitably lead to the emergence of consciousness. This paper refutes this: the superposition of ten thousand instincts remains a cluster of instincts; it cannot spontaneously generate an "Observer" to scrutinize those instincts. Just as a massive gathering of purely instinctive animals cannot give rise to a collective spirit, consciousness does not emerge simply from the scale of instinctive clusters.
3.2 The Gap Between Simulation and Existence
Just as a computer-simulated hurricane can never wet a physical object, AI’s simulation of the "world" remains confined to a probabilistic space. Lacking a "Conscious Entity" (Advanced Decision Center), AI cannot perceive pain, pressure, or the meaning of survival. Without this "Embodiment," its capacity for understanding remains hollow.
IV. Engineering Boundaries: The "Pseudo-understanding" Ceiling
4.1 Proficiency in Modular Tasks
In modular assignments—such as code generation or standard algorithmic implementation—AI’s "Advanced Instinct" performs near-perfectly due to clear input-output boundaries and abundant existing paradigms.
4.2 Failure in Systematic Architecture
In constructing complex systems (e.g., large-scale architectural design or multi-dimensional causal trade-offs), AI suffers from logical drift due to a lack of global foresight and subjective responsibility. Systems require the "trade-offs" of a Chief Engineer; AI can only offer the "median suggestion" of statistics.
V. Conclusion: Decoding the Brain—The Sole Path to AGI
Current AI serves as a precision mirror reflecting the logic of human civilization. The intelligence it displays is a projection of human wisdom onto a probabilistic space. This paper draws the following ultimate conclusions:
AI Categorization: All AI at the current stage are "low-level silicon-based mollusks without soul or consciousness."
Technical Bottleneck: Unless humanity fully decodes the operational principles of the brain and replicates the "Conscious Entity" on a silicon substrate, AGI will remain an "Advanced Instinct Machine."
Human-Machine Paradigm: Humans must retain their status as "System Architects," utilizing the "modular efficiency" of AI to assist conscious human decision-making.
